Gibson SJ-100

The SJ 100 was apparently a very popular guitar and considered a good value for the money. It had mahogany back and sides, spruce top (the above SJ 100 had an Adirondack top), a bound body and bound maple neck, two pearl dots on the bridge, and ebony fingerboard with pearl dot inlays, and kluson tuners.
